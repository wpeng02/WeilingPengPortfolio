WindowSill

A decorative mobile game revolves around your windowsill to attract birds.

Brief

WindowSill is a cartoon-style casual mobile game. In the game, different birds have different needs. The player provides food and water, customizes the windowsill with toy and plant decorations to meet the birds’ needs and attract them to visit. The game aims to provide a simple relaxing virtual bird watching experience that relieves stress while educating players about wild birds.

Game Design

I interviewed bird lovers who set up feeders on their balconies or in their backyard to feed birds in winter. To them, the surprise of opening the window every morning and seeing different visitors is most delightful—which is what I wanted the players to experience in my game.


The gameplay design takes inspiration from other casual decorative games: Neko Atsume and Abyssrium.

Frame Sketches

Style

I created a hand-drawn quality to the overall style of the game, to provide a light-hearted, relaxing interface. I designed the background with limited elements and an earth-colored palette to make the birds stand out. I used wood patterns to give the setting a natural atmosphere. The design of the birds is in cartoon. I increased the saturation and color contrast and exaggerated size differences to highlight each species’ prominent characteristics.

Feeding system

There are four types of food (seeds, fruit, insect, meat) that attract different kinds of birds. Birds visit if the food provided is to their liking.

Decoration system

The comfort level of the place is sustained by three qualities—security, playfulness, cleanliness. Players decorate the windowsill with toys to increase the playfulness, plants to increase the security, and clean up after the birds to maintain cleanliness. Birds visit only if the three qualities of the place meet their minimum needs.

Reward system

After birds visit, they leave gifts which they exchange for a currency to purchase more bird food and decorations.

Weather system

Three weather conditions in the game affect birds’ visits. On sunny days birds are most attracted to playfulness; on rainy days birds are most attracted to security; on snowy days birds are the most attracted to food.

Collecting system

The Bird Journal documents birds’ basic information after their first visit and the photos players took during the game.


Players can learn more with the Birdpedia function which provides real photos and basic information to help identify these birds in the world.

Bonus features

To add a little challenge to the game, I designed unexpected visitors who appear randomly as a distraction—a squirrel that steals bird food and a cat that scares birds away. Players can send them away with the snack they ask for (or wait until they leave).


To increase the playability of the game, I added several “magical” items in the game, such as weather dolls hung up to change the weather, or good luck charm to increase the chance of rare birds in a given time period.
