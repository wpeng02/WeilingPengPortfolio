Checkerboard Redesign for Color-blindness

Sternhalma (Chinese Checker) redesign that is more friendly to people with colorblind-ness

Brief

Chinese Checker is a strategy board game (originated in German), played by two to six players. The objective is to be first to race all of one's pieces across the hexagram-shaped board into the opposite corner. The checker pieces are usually six different colored sets of marbles. In this project, I redesigned the color palette and form of the checker pieces to make them more distinguishable for 4 types of color-blindness.

Problem

The game heavily relies on distinguishing colors—the six sets of marbles differ only in colors. The traditional color palette (white, yellow, green, blue, red, black) may cause confusion for people with color-blindness: red and green are similar to Portanopia, blue and green are similar to Tritanopia, white and yellow are similar to Complete Color Blindness. In the game when the checker pieces of different colors encounters at the center of the board, it becomes evern harder to distinguish.

Redesign the Color Palette

In order to avoid the indistinguishable colors for color-blindness such as red with green, and blue with green, I avoid the use of green in the new color palette, and enhance the distinction with different levels of brightness. The most contrasting color pairs are positioned in the opposite corners because the two opposite groups of checker pieces will intersect the most in the game play.

Redesign the Form

To further enhance the distinction between each set, I redesigned the form—giving each set a recognizable simple geometric form, with line patterns in contrasting colors.
