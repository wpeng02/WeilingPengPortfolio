Stripeio

A set of cutting board for children to learn cutting with real kitchen knife

Brief

Stripeio is a set of cutting boards that bridge the gap between culinary toys and tools, designed for kids from 6 to 11 to practice using a real kitchen knife in a safe and joyful way.

User Research

In our online interview, 40 kids from 5-8 interested in cooking were asked to rate the difficulties in learning chopping with a real kitchen knife. The highest rated includes:


·Knife controling(some mentioned they can’t hold round items on cutting board);

·Correct hand position(cannot hold items with their guide hand in the claw position).


Vegetables that are round, easy-to-roll, firm-textured (such as potatoes, carrots, and radishes) are their least favorite to chop.

Market Research

Few products in the market can help kids transition from cutting with a toy knife to a real knife.


Most culinary toys are designed to mimic the cooking process. The most common practice knives in the market are plastic saw blade knives that work differently than real knives.

Goal

Design a product that prevents vegetables from rolling on cutting board to help children focusing on knife handling, bridging the gap between toys and real knives.





Design




Functionality


I designed a cutting board with a concave V-shape structure provides stable support to help children holding things still on cutting board. The vertical slots are for children to practice cutting in even spaces.

Playfulness


To make the design fun and engaging for children, the cutting boards are designed in shapes of three animal with stirpes: bee, zebra and tropical fish. The heads, limbs, tails, and wings are removable pieces that children can build different looking characters with different assembling combinations.
