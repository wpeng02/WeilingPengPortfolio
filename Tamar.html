Tamar

A series of food product designs based on a hero ingredient: tamarind.

Brief

TAMAR—a series of vegan, low-calorie, tamarind-based sauces served with main dishes and dessert–is a new approach to the traditional sauce in both rich sour flavor and playful packaging design.

Goal

This is a three-person collaborative project developing a series of food products based on a hero ingredient of our own choice. We picked tamarind for its various health benefits, unique sour taste, and the potential for innovation because of its rarity in American food culture.


(Team member: Abisola Ashamu, Siddharth Thuppil)

User Group

Based on research on the preference of sourness in different age groups in the US, we targeted the user group on Gen Z (age from 10-25) for their highest sour preference and open mind to new things.

Product Development: Flavor

We decided on the sauce as the final form of our product because it had the best flavor and widest utility. We developed two flavors: Golden Masala (savory) served with rice, protein, and salad, and Mixed Berry (Sweet) served with desserts.



Savory: Golden Masala

The Golden Masala was inspired by the traditional Indian masala sauce. We arrived at the flavor by making the tartness and earthiness of tamarind shine through the other spices in the traditional recipe.

Sweet: Mixed Berry

While the Mixed Berry flavor has a different focus, functioning as a palate cleanser, using the strong sourness of the fruit to balance the sweetness of the desserts. We experimented with different kinds of fruit and found that the fruity and grassy flavor of berries worked the best with the earthiness of tamarind.

Product Development: Form

Our goal is to provide users a pleasurable experience by adding the sauce in a single serving pouch that they cut open on their food, like cutting open a benedict egg on a muffin. To achieve the goal, we used the spherification technique of Molecular gastronomy.

Product Development: Branding and Packaging

We designed an egg-shaped container as individual packaging for each pouch. The user can easily peel off the sticker, and pour out the sauce ball. The patterns on the sticker indicate the ingredients and flavor.
